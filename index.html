GEM3D: Generative Medial Abstractions for 3D Shape Synthesis

Dmitry Petrov1, Pradyumn Goyal1, Vikas Thamizharasan1, Vladimir G. Kim2, Matheus Gadelha2, Melinos Averkiou3, Siddhartha Chaudhuri2, Evangelos Kalogerakis1
1University of Massachusetts, Amherst 2Adobe Research 3University of Cyprus
Paper arXiv Code Data

GEM3D generates 3D shapes in two steps: first, it generates medial skeleton (in red); second, it generates surface (in green) following skeleton structure.

Abstract

We introduce GEM3D — a new deep, topology-aware generative model of 3D shapes.

The key ingredient of our method is a neural skeleton-based representation encoding information on both shape topology and geometry. Through a denoising diffusion probabilistic model, our method first generates skeleton-based representations following the Medial Axis Transform (MAT), then generates surfaces through a skeleton-driven neural implicit formulation. The neural implicit takes into account the topological and geometric information stored in the generated skeleton representations to yield surfaces that are more topologically and geometrically accurate compared to previous neural field formulations.

We discuss applications of our method in shape synthesis and point cloud reconstruction tasks, and evaluate our method both qualitatively and quantitatively. We demonstrate significantly more faithful surface reconstruction and diverse shape generation results compared to the state-of-the-art, also involving challenging scenarios of reconstructing and synthesizing structurally complex, high-genus shape surfaces from Thingi10K and ShapeNet.

Approach

GEM3D architecture: starting with 3D Gaussian noise, our model first diffusion stage generates a point-based medial (skeletal) shape representation conditioned on a shape category embedding. Conditioned on this representation, our second diffusion stage generates latent codes capturing shape information around the medial points. In the last stage, our surface decoder decodes the medial latent codes and points to local neural implicit surface representations, which are then aggregated to create an output 3D shape.

Surface sampling

Surface sampling conditioned on generated skeleton. Our model can sample diverse and structurally consistent surfaces from generated skeletons with meaningful variations of local geometries like different cockpits for airplane or different shapes of lamp stands.

Generation from user-modeled skeletons

Surface generation based on user-modeled skeletons. Our model is able to generalize to unseen structures encoded in user-provided skeletons, and produces plausible surface samples from them.

Some related Links

Shape reconstruction and generation through irregular latents grids of local neural implicits. Our 3D representation can be seen as irregular latent grid that is arranged on medial abstraction (skeleton). This line of work was introduced recently in Dynamic Code Clouds and 3DILG papers have shown that irregular latent grids either dynamically arranged in space (Dynamic Code Clouds) or uniformly sampled on surface (3DILG) provide strong representations that are able to capture fine details of geometry and part articulation. 3DShape2VecSet, a follow-up work for 3DILG, have shown that latents on irregular latent grid don't need to have an associated 3D position and also introduced strong latent diffusion model based on EDM diffusion approach.

Skeletonization and skeleton-guided surface representation. We draw heavy inspiration from 3D skeletonization, the computation of medial skeletons from a surface representation, that is a well-studied problem in geometry processing. Our skeleton inference model for shape reconstruction from point clouds is heavily inspired by P2P-Net. Finally, exciting concurrent work Medial Skeletal Diagram discusses in great mathematical detail an idea of 'generalized enveloping primitive', a concept that represents the shape's surface as a directional distance field around the medial axis.
